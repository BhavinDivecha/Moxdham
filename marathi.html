Important actions to be taken till 13th day after death of a person

How to bury a child?

After death Rites:

  • The scriptures say that after death, the bodies of children (evidence) should be mined and the bodies of the elderly (adults) should be cremated. The formulas for the funeral of children and the scriptures about them are given below.
    1. If the child dies before naming (up to 12th day) mining should be done. (Physical evidence.)
    2. After the death of a child up to 3 years of age with or without a rite of passage, either burning or mining is done. However, it is more appropriate to bury after death if it is not completed after 2 years, whereas it is more suitable to be cremated after death if it is completed after 2 years.
  • The science behind burying the body of a child and cremating the body of an adult
    1. The gross body is related to the earth. A child of 2 years old has a thick body. Therefore, it can easily mix with the gross body (earth principle) in the soil (earth principle), that is, the decomposition of the gross body into the five elements is easy. As we age, the inertia of the gross and subtle bodies increases. If the body is buried, it is difficult to disintegrate in Panchatattva. After death, his body was reduced to ashes by cremation. This ash mixes easily in the soil, which means that the gross body is easily decomposed into five elements.
    2. The virtues and vices of the body are attached to the mind of the elderly. If such a body is buried, the penis, which is attached to the gross body, is less likely to be buried there. '
