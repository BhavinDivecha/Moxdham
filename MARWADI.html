Funeral

There are sixteen sacraments in Hinduism from birth to death. In these, the last ie the sixteenth rites are the rites that take place after death, which include the last farewell of the person, cremation and rituals that bring peace to the soul. The last rites have been given a lot in the scriptures because by this a person gets the best place in the hereafter and in the next life birth and happiness in the best family. It has been told in the Garuda Purana that the soul of a person whose last rites is not performed, after death, wanders as a ghost and suffers various kinds of troubles. Death is not considered the end of life in Hinduism. Upon death, it is believed that this is the time when the soul leaves this body and again assumes a new form, or begins the journey to attain salvation. This is the reason behind cremating the dead body after the death of a person. After coming out of the dead body, the soul is given speed on the path of salvation. It is believed in the scriptures that until the last rites of the dead body are not performed, he does not attain salvation. Cremation is the last rites out of the sixteen rites to be performed according to the scriptures. This ritual is performed in two ways.

1. When dead body is available. He should be lit on fire with complete law.

2. When a person dies by starvation, by violent animal, by hanging, by venom, by fire, by suicide, by falling, by rope, by keeping in water, by snakebite, by lightning, by iron, by weapon If it is caused by touching the mouth of a poisonous dog, then it is called Durmaran. In all these situations, death is not considered normal and the effigy cremation method is used for cremation after death.

Cremation does not happen after sunset Cremation is never done after sunset. If the death took place after sunset, then it can be burnt only in the morning of the next day. It is believed that by performing cremation after sunset, the soul of the deceased person has to suffer in the afterlife and there may be defects in any part of it in the next life.

At the time of cremation, the body placed on the pyre is circumambulated by taking water in a pitcher with a hole and at the end it is thrown back and broken. This action is done to dislodge the soul of the dead person from his body. But a deep philosophical mystery is also hidden in this action. This means that life is like a pot with a hole in which the water of age keeps on dripping every moment and in the end the soul leaves everything and the life of the pot ends.

Pind Daan is performed by the male relatives of the deceased person. At the time of cremation, the heads of the male relatives of the deceased are shaved. It is not only a means of expressing reverence to the dead person, it also means that the responsibility has now come on them.
